As the data controller, we are responsible for your personal information and are committed to handling it with care, integrity and full compliance with applicable data protection legislation, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) for California residents. 3. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you submit a consultation request, fill in a contact form, send us an email or otherwise communicate with us directly. The categories of personal data we may collect include: your full name and preferred form of address; your email address and telephone number; your residential or project address, including city, state and country; the approximate size of your property; your estimated budget and preferred timeline; details about your design preferences, inspiration and objectives; any images, mood boards or links you choose to share with us; and any other information you voluntarily include in your communications with us.

We may also collect certain technical data automatically when you visit our website. This may include your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed and the date and time of your visit. This data is collected using cookies and similar tracking technologies and is used solely for the purposes of improving the functionality and performance of our website.

We use the personal information you provide for the following purposes: to respond promptly and appropriately to your consultation inquiries; to assess the scope and nature of your project and determine whether our services are suitable for your needs; to provide, manage and deliver our interior design services throughout the duration of a project; to communicate with you regarding project updates, timelines, material selections and other relevant matters; to prepare and send proposals, contracts, invoices and other project documentation; to coordinate with trusted third-party suppliers, contractors and craftspeople as required to complete your project; to comply with our legal and contractual obligations; and to improve and develop our services, website and business operations.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Where applicable under the GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases for processing your personal data: your consent, where you have freely and clearly given it; the performance of a contract, where processing is necessary to deliver the services you have requested; compliance with a legal obligation, where processing is required by law; and our legitimate interests, where processing is necessary for the purposes of our business operations, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights and interests. We do not sell, rent, trade or otherwise disclose your personal information to third parties for their own marketing or commercial purposes. All third parties with whom we share your data are required to implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures and to process your data only in accordance with our instructions and applicable data protection law.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. For prospective clients who have submitted an inquiry but have not proceeded to engage our services, we will retain your information for a period of up to twelve months, after which it will be securely deleted. Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. We use only essential cookies, which are necessary for the website to function correctly. We do not use advertising, targeting or third-party tracking cookies. You may configure your browser settings to refuse or delete cookies at any time; however, please note that doing so may affect the functionality of certain areas of our website. By continuing to use our website, you consent to the use of essential cookies in accordance with this policy.
